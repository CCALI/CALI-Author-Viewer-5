Why should there ever be any knowledge requirement?

The drafters of subchapter F took the sensible position that, if the director was unaware of the interest at the time of the transaction, there was no reason to worry about any conflict of interest. If the director doesn't know that he or a related person has a financial interest in a transaction, it won't affect his behavior. (Technically, they didn't require in ยง 8.60(1)(i) that the director have knowledge when he's a party to the transaction, but it's hard to imagine many situations where a director will be a party to a transaction and not know it.)

For example, if I tell you to rate me by sliding the slider below, and don't tell you that you will receive $1 million for rating me "wonderful", the $1 million won't affect you. It's only if you know about the financial interest that your behavior is suspect.
